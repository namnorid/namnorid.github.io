SOP: Standard Operating Procedure
Start

Begin by collecting the equipment specified in 5S. After the pre-production meeting go to the sorting track (figure 1) and to the infeed you were assigned according to the daily schedule.

Ordinary work

1.Remove the plastic wrapping of the pallet and always put it directly in to the plastic waste compressor.

2.Prioritize placing export goods on the sorting path.

3.It is very important that the package's Bring label is not placed with the label downwards.

4.Sort out packages over 120cm long, over 60cm wide or weighing over 35kg. Place them in the EMB cage (fig. 2) next to your assigned Infeed.

5.Look out for split pallet with many packages to the same destination. Try to produce these at intervals with packages to other locations. Pick packages from another pallet if needed.

Consider the following:

•Very important that you keep track of the contents of the package as we may never place liquids such as wine / beer / oil etc. on the sorting path. Should similar liquids leak out, the chances are considerable that mechanical or electrical parts will be destroyed. Add packages of liquids to the EMB cage.

•Think about your lifting technique! Neutral back and bent legs are important to avoid wear and tear. We have suction plugs for handling heavy packages, use these correctly, so you will not lose efficiency.

•If you see that the feed path stops before the yellow arc, keep a greater distance between the packages you place.

•Should you see that there are four blue lights or two flashing blue lights on the sorting path warning system for the yield level, stop placing for 2-3 minutes or until the lights have gone out.

•Packages with objects that protrude from the packaging or do not lie stable against the track, are also placed in the EMB cage.

•Remember to not unwrap all 4 pallets at the same time, as this creates an uneven flow for the sorting path.

•Produce the pallets from right to left, as far as possible.

•It is important that you keep a good pace especially with the unwrapping, in order to avoid bottlenecks.

•Always place EMB packages directly in the EMB cage. Do not leave them on the pallet / cage on the infeed surfaces.

•It is important that you tell the pallet forwarder if you are tired and want to change.

•Placing is physically exhausting; it is good if you have drinks to consume during the work shift.

End of production

After production, you should assist with cleaning Infeed, unless otherwise stated by the Team Leader. When all cars are departed, go into the office and check which cleaning area you have been allocated. Finish the area according to the cleaning instructions.
