Responsible: Daniel Namnori
Assignment: Chutes sorting
Section: HUB – Production – Packages
Process: Package sorting
Updated: 2019-09-04
SOP: Standard Operating Procedure
Start

Begin by retrieving the equipment specified in 5S. After the pre-production meeting, go to the sorting path (fig. 1) and to the area you have been allocated according to the current staffing schedule.

Ordinary work

Check that cages and pallets are available, and the outer cage is open. Be aware that the cage is secured. Make sure the pallets have a little space between them and are in the marked area. Always build the cage in order, row by row. Broken cages are to be put in the truck lane and the Cage drivers are notified. If the cage flags run out, notify the Cage drivers.

Check the zip code (fig. 2.) Zip code sorting is found above the chute (fig 3.) Wrong sorted packages are placed under the chute. Pay attention to chutes with multiple sorting directions. Packages that can’t be identified should be placed under the chute. Dangerous goods/LQ to Visby (62) is placed under the chute (fig. 5.) Evaluate the size of the package. Small packages and packages that do not fit on pallets, as well as heavy packages are placed in cages. Packages that are ¼ or ½ pallets to size are sorted on pallet. Evaluate after packaging. NOTE! Exceptions exist.

Make sure the pallet is intact. Heavy packages in the bottom. No package may be placed outside the pallet (if necessary, the pallet may be longer, not wider!) First pack the packages on half the pallet at the far end, to save space on the pallet for heavy packages. Build in view of the center of gravity of the finished pallet.

Think about your lifting technique! Neutral back and bent legs are important to avoid wear and tear. Sorting is physically fatiguing. It’s good if you have any drink to consume.

Build the cage from the inside and out. Save half of the cage floor for heavy packages. Stack the packages firmly and with minimal air between the packages (fig. 4.) Think of Tetris. Place packages into the cage, do not throw them.

Finish the cage and mark it with the correct destination flag. Make sure the cage is closed. Old cage flags must be thrown out. Put the cage in the truck aisle with the correct flag. When a pallet is complete, place a flag between two packages outwards towards the forklift aisle. Communicate with the Cage drivers when pallet / cage is complete.

End of production

After production, you should help with wrapping the remaining pallets, and close the remaining cages. When the trucks are departed, go to the office and check what cleaning area you received. Thoroughly clean the area according to the cleaning instructions. Ask the cleaning supervisor where help is needed.
