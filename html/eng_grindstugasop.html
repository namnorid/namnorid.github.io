SOP: Standard Operating Procedure
Start

Begin by collecting the equipment listed in 5S. After the pre-production meeting you go to the gatehouse. Once in place, remove all window covers and start the computer for gate control. Turn on the TV that shows the terminal yard and the terminal via camera surveillance. Open the gates on the gate computer by pressing the top pane and bottom pane until it turns yellow in the system, then the gates open. Start the Citrix on the other computer and log in to the system. Go into the file system and find Night Report, select today's date from traffic reports at F:\Bring\Parcels\PR Production\PR Jönköping\PR Hub\Nattrapport\. Check your e-mail if there is something special that must be considered when it comes to the traffic for the night. Always wear a vest when working outdoors if you are wearing reflective clothing.

Ordinary work

When trucks arrive, ask the driver which route he drives or from which city they arrive from. For example, if ORB-JKG1 arrives, log the arrival time in the Domestic Inbound tab in the traffic report and that ORB-JKG1 arrived at XX:XX (Figure 1.) Ask the driver if most of the volumes are export volumes, and loaded on either the car or the trailer. If the entire truck has a majority of export volumes, it is unloaded in gate 3-10. If the truck has a majority of export volumes on either the car or trailer, let the part of the truck unload the export volume section in port 3-10. The part of the truck that has Parcel volumes is guided into gates 49–55 for unloading. The largest proportion of cars is unloaded only in gates 49–55. Tell the driver which gates to unload in. If it is a new driver, provide written safety instructions that apply to the terminal and terminal area.

Note! Remind the driver to block the car and trailer after docking at the gate.

Make sure drivers respect the queuing system and wait for their turn at unloading. If the truck is sealed, we open the barrier by pressing the barrier button on the barrier / gate computer so that that button turns yellow for one second and asks the driver to drive past the booms. Break the seal by cutting it with the pliers. The number on the seal is checked by checking the seal number to match the driver's "CMR" or other shipping letters.

At gates 3–10 and primarily gates 49–55, a queuing system is used to keep the flow of goods to the terminal at an even level. When a truck arrives at gate 49–55 or 3–10, the unloaders report how many pallet places are loaded on each truck. Enter the number of pallet places of each truck by typing in the linehaul report in the tabs Domestic Inbound and Import respectively. Make sure you specify both car and trailer in the report. Communication with the Unloading personnel is done by using the Motorola Walkie talkie on channel 8. The walkie talkie is turned on by spinning the button on the top. During the beginning of the production, it is good to test the Walkie talkie to ensure that the staff at the terminal hears the information well. Use the Motorola Walkie talkie on Channel 7 to reach the export loading, as well as the HD loading.

When Loaders 1-3 and Export loaders report the number of pallet places (Figure 2), this should be reported in the linehaul report in the tabs for domestic output and export respectively. Make sure the numbers for both the car and the trailer are provided. Take your break when the unloading staff goes on their break. Be sure to clean the gate before you go in accordance with the instructions in 5S, and that all windows and door are closed properly. Continue to record all pallet places and times on the truck arriving or departing on a listing block, and then fill in the linehaul report. After the break you will use the usual Motorola Walkie talkie for all communication.

Drivers may not take their rest in the terminal yard; send them to the parking area at Statoil.

End of production

After the break, the others at the terminal help with tasks that must be done before the cars leave. Ask your Team leader if you don't know who needs help. When the production at EMB and sorting is complete start with the checklist (Figure 3.) Check all items on the list and when this is done, inform the terminal staff that we can send all domestic cars. Go to the office and fill in the last departures, save the traffic report. Help the others with the final cleaning of their areas. Do not forget to return the equipment used during the night (Walkie-talkies, forklift, etc.). Ask Cleaning supervisor where help is needed on the cleaning.
