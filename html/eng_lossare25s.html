Unloader 2 5S

Responsible: Bekim Marniku
Assignment: Unloading
Section: HUB – Production – Pallets
Process: Incoming goods
Updated: 2019-09-18
5S: Sort, Set in order, Shine, Standardize, Sustain om
Equipment

-  Clothing: Regular Bring uniform. Extra warm layer and jacket if needed. Safety gloves and safety shoes.

-  Communication equipment: Motorola walkie-talkie marked with number 9. The Motorola walkie-talkie is charged at the office.

-  Computers: Four computers for public use are located at the office.

-  Forklift: High lift forklift marked with Unloading 2, this is located at the forklift recharge station. Replacement batteries are available if required. Make sure to change the battery prior reaching 20%. Clean the truck from garbage after production. Water the truck if the battery power exceeds 90%. After production, the forklift must be charged. If the forklift is broken, notify Niclas Klahr of which type of damage have occurred and attach a damage-flag to the forklift to indicate that the forklift is damaged and may not be used. Ask Team Leader which forklift to use if the primary forklift is unavailable.

-  Other equipment: ballpoint pen, knife, marker (fig. 2.)

-  If equipment is missing, contact your Team leader.

Arbetsstation

The primary workstation is at gates 49-56 on the terminal. Open the doors on the terminal by placing your card against the card reader and enter your password if necessary. During production, you will need to sign shipping notes to drivers; Custom papers to Oslo1 NOO-JKG1 are given to the pallet drivers who leave them to the Customs. The remaining papers are thrown into the trash bin at gate 55. Gate 49-56 should be free from packages / cages / pallets during production, remove these before unloading. If garbage from pallet is located on the terminal floor, these must be removed and placed between the gatehouses and preferably in the trash baskets. The wheels of the forklifts will wear hard if we do not remove them. The pallets may only be placed in each specific area due to the risk of poor visibility.

Cleaning

After production, you clean the area you have received on the list of cleaning positions. Cleaning zones may vary; always check the cleaning list.
Daily cleaning on cleaning zone 1 (fig. 1) 'Shortside, arrivals + gatehouses':

1.  Drive away all goods from the track to their respective area.

2.  Drive empty pallets to the T2 track. Stack the pallets neatly.

3.  Drive the empty cages from the arriving side to the compartments between the sort path's chute.

4.  Cleaning of the area between the gates and gatehouses from pallet debris and paper; sweep it out of the gates.

5.  Sweep the area at the return.

5.  Fill the compartments with cages.

5.  Help others with their respective cleaning areas.

6.  Drive the yellow bin together with zone 8; Gather the garbage heaps around the sorter and empty the bucket in the compactor outside port 12.
