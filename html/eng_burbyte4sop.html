Cage driving 4 SOP

Responsible: Daniel Namnori
Assignment: Package sorting
Section: HUB – Production – Sorting
Process: Pallet wrapping - Loading
Updated: 2019-10-22
SOP: Standard Operating Procedure
Start

Begin by retrieving the equipment specified in 5S. After the pre-production meeting, drive to the departures side (Figure 1) and make sure the track is built. Each chute must have two pallets. Broken cages are placed at the security cage. Remove old markings and attach new straps to the cages.

Ordinary work

When the pallet is finished, check that it is in full height. Does the split pallets have more space, fill up with packages from the associated cage. Drive the pallet from the sorter to a place where you are visible to forklift traffic. Flag the pallet using the stapler. Wrap the pallet from the bottom edge to the top packages. Then place the pallet at the correct gate / surface close to other load carriers or load pallet if truck is inside the port. Always perform a visual check of some packages before you mark / flag the pallet with the intended DIP. When wrapping the pallet, always check that the plastic is well-tense due to the safety of the loading. If you are unsure if you can load, then ask the departures / arriving staff.

When cages are finished / full, make sure it is well stocked before rolling it out to the forklift aisle. Flag the cage with the correct flag. Always perform a visual inspection of the goods before you flag the cage. Drive the cage to the correct port / surface and place the pallets close together (Figure 2.) If available, load the cage directly on the truck. Make sure that cages against red-marked DIPs are below cage height. You must not drive with truck in to the chutes and pick up cages.

Cage driving 4 shall also help the Forwarder. Mainly Infeed 4, and mainly cages.

End of production

Once the tracks have departed, go to the office and check what cleaning area you received. Thoroughly clean the area according to the cleaning instructions. Do not forget to return the equipment used during the night (Walkie talkies, forklift etc.). Ask cleaning supervisor where help might be needed.
