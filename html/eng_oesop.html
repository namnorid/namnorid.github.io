SOP: Standard Operating Procedure
Start

Begin by collecting the OE computer and the stapler in the office. Then retrieve the packages in the retention shelf. Set up all equipment (computers, labeling, pallets, etc.) so production can begin. Make sure packages to Norway that are on the shelf are placed in yesterday’s single cages. Communicate with the Forwarder that they can start producing export goods on the sorting machine.

Ordinary work

1.  Between 20:15 and 23:15, OE shall as far as possible ensure that the export goods from the split square, are produced. Keep an eye out so the Forwarder priorities export goods.

2.  The break is usually between 23:00 and 23:30. After the break, it is important to check EMB’s finished goods for OE goods.

3.  At 23:30, produced split pallets shall be completed in CBM, see fig. 1.

•Lehman, COD DK, Itella, DE shall be closed in CBM Sunday-Thursday.

•Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania shall be closed on Tuesdays and Thursdays.

•All other destinations must be closed every day.

4.  When all shipments have been completed and moved to their respective area, start producing the single packages to Norway, see OE guide *.

5.  For problem packages, write on a post-it note and attach to the shipment; what kind of error that our customs department should look for.

6.  When the single packages are completed, check whether EMB has more OE goods for you to produce.

Keep in mind the following:

•  Always prioritize chutes before other handling.

•  Always label goods well so that no mistakes are made due to poor labeling.

•  Make sure to always scan packages in CBM directly you have the opportunity.

•  You can close more pallets after 00:00 (if the amount of goods is available.)

•  Do not forget that you are responsible for transporting all the finished pallets to their respective area (FI included.)

•  *The OE guide: F:\Bring\Parcels\PR Production\PR Jönköping\PR Hub\Lean\Lathundar och arbetsbeskrivningar\

End of production

When all export packages are produced, start with your cleaning. Make sure you have prepared for the next business day; there must be extra corrugated cardboard containers and pallets. Do not forget to scan goods on pallets which are to be closed on the same day. Put all equipment in the correct positions, see 5S. OE has the responsibility to refill cage flags in the shelfs at the gate computer (next to EMB.)
